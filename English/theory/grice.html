Grice’s Maxims (1975)

Grice said that when we communicate, we assume that we subconsciously will attempt to be conversationally cooperative, even if we don’t want to be socially cooperative.

For example, if people are arguing, they will still be displaying the “conversation rules”:

Conversation Rules

  • Quantity – The most helpful amount of information
  • Quality – How true the information is
  • Relation – How relevant the information is
  • Manner – How the information is presented

How can these rules be broken

He said that these Maxims can be either flouted, or violated

Flouting

The maxim can be broken, but in a way that the other person still understands what you are trying to convey, such as when using sarcasm. This is still cooperative.

Violating

The maxim can be completely broken, where it is obvious that they are not being cooperative in the slightest, and the hearer only gets the surface meaning of the utterance. This is deliberately uncooperative, while flouting can be accidental.
